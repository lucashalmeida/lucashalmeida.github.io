Why should we work together?

Five years ago, I came to Canada to work as a software developer. I had just finished my Bachelor of Information Systems and I was very excited about this opportunity. Working full-time while finishing my degree, I thought I knew what to expect in my new career, but I could never have imagined how much I would end up learning.
Shortly after I arrived in Canada, I was invited to join a new startup initiative inside the company that I was working for. From that moment on, I became very interested in everything related to tech startups. I was reading everything I could find about engineering best practices, code quality, UX, UI, design, company culture, business strategies, and marketing. I tried to wear as many hats as possible.
As a software developer, I learned a lot about web development, REST APIs and different Python frameworks. I worked on every part of the product, from databases to product onboarding. In the beginning, I was one of two software developers, so I had to learn how to get the job done. With time, I found myself really focusing on front-end work, where I was able to improve how the product looked and performed, while making it easier to use. I acquired many skills in this position and learned what it really means to be a full stack developer.
In 2019 I decided to look for a new opportunity, because I felt that I had overgrown my current position. I wanted to be more involved in the creative process and work on business strategy and leadership. With this change, I decided to work for a bigger startup, to experience a different stage of a company's journey. That's when I joined Auvik Networks. At Auvik, I work on the Presentation Team, which is responsible for our APIs, UI, and Integrations. Here, I have had the opportunity to really solidify my technical knowledge, and see many of the best practices I had read about.
For my next career move, I want to either start my own startup, or join a small one, like Tailwind UI. I believe that the experience I acquired in my early career allows me to be a great contributor in both technical and non-technical areas. I love to solve technical problems, but I also love to bounce ideas about illustration, audio synthesis, leadership, and any other creative areas.
Latelly, I've been writing React components with Storybook, which is an incredible tool. The way it allows developers to isolate components, and unit test them is amazing. I believe that most companies would benefit from having a reusable component system in place. However, it makes me wonder how we could have high level document definitions, such as JSON, that can be used to customize such components. For instance, we can have a component for a grid, but each use of this grid specifies different types of columns, so each grid becomes a new component, and it requires several hundred lines of code. The high level definitions may reduce this work.
