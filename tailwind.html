Why should we work together?

Five years ago I came to Canada to work as a software developer. I was fresh out of school. Being a full time worker while finishing my degree, I thought I knew what to expect next. But I could never have imagined how much I would end up learning.
As soon I arrived in Canada, I was invited to join a new initiative inside the company that hired me. We started a new company. From that moment on, I became very interested in everything related to tech startups. I was reading everything about engineering best practices, code quality, UX, UI, design, company culture, business strategies, and marketing. I was trying to wear as many hats as possible. At first, I succeeded as a software developer. But then, my ideas for other areas were suppressed one by one. The work became tedious, and the company never reached any success. Despite the overall company difficulties, I acquired many skills and learned what it really means to be a full stack developer. In the beginning, we were only two software developers, so I had to learn how to get the job done.
In 2019 I decided to leave, because I felt that my contributions were no longer significant. With this change, I decided to work for a bigger startup, to experience a different stage of a company's journey. That's when I joined Auvik Networks. At Auvik, I work in the Presentation Team, which is responsible for our APIs, UI, and Integrations. Here, I had the opportunity to really solidify my technical knowledge, and see many of the best practices I read about being used.
For my next career move, I want to either start my own startup, or join a small one, like Tailwind UI. I believe that the experience I acquired in my early career allows me to be a great contributor in both technical and non-technical areas. I love to solve technical problems, but I also love to bounce ideas about anything that gets my attention, such as illustration, audio synthesis, or leadership.
Latelly, I've been writing React components with Storybook, which is an incredible tool. The way it allows developers to isolate components, and unit test them is amazing. I believe that most companies would benefit from having a reusable component system in place. However, it makes me wonder how we could have high level document definitions, such as JSON, that can be used to customize such components. For instance, we can have a component for a grid, but each use of this grid specifies different types of columns, so each grid becomes a new component, and it requires several hundred lines of code. The high level definitions may reduce this work.
